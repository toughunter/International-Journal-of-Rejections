About the Journal

Aim and scope

Traditional academic publishing suffers from the file-drawer problem. Countless hours of rigorous research—from negative results to highly specific field evaluations—are abandoned due to subjective editorial gatekeeping.

This journal operates on a radical premise: Rejection is a shared academic reality, not a measure of scientific invalidity. We accept manuscripts that have been formally rejected elsewhere, publishing them alongside their anonymized peer-review comments and the author's final, unfiltered rebuttal.

Instructions

Guide for authors

  • Anonymize everything: You must redact the names of the journals and editors who rejected you. Replace them with `[Redacted]`.
  • The Rebuttal: Keep it professional, or don't. This is your space to explain why the review was flawed, contradictory, or missed the point.
  • The Manuscript: Attach the final PDF version of the paper as it was submitted. We do not require you to reformat citations.
